We will start our exploration of the self by looking at a couple of the world's dominant paradigms.

The persepective we are probably familiar with is the Western conception of self. Many of these foundations of the Western self come from Christian theology. For example, the idea of an everlasting soul separate from the body, the idea that an individual will face judgement from an omnicient deity after their death, and that the soul will spend eternity in either heaven or hell following its time in the world. Other aspects of the western self come from economic and political conditions. Democracy and Capitalism are a couple of the driving inspirations for the self In America.
